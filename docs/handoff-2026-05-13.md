## RF-1: Watterson channel model — what was fixed and why

**Background**: benchmark runs showed BPSK250 achieves 20/20 at AWGN 15 dB and 20/20 at Watterson Good F1, but 0/20 at Watterson Good F2 with every FEC mode. Suspected TED/PLL failure. Investigation revealed two bugs in the *channel model*, not the demodulator.

**Bug 1 — Independent fading per 1024-sample block**
`crates/openpulse-channel/src/watterson.rs` regenerated completely independent fading envelopes at each 1024-sample block boundary. A 255-byte RS-encoded BPSK250 frame is ~66 k samples → 65 discontinuous channel jumps within one `apply()` call. This is physically wrong (real ionospheric fading is continuous).

Fix: generate one FFT-sized envelope per `apply()` call using `fft_size = next_power_of_two(signal_len)`, giving proper temporal correlation over the full frame.

**Bug 2 — Magnitude-only fading creates a static frequency-domain null**
Using `h.norm()` discards the complex Gaussian's random phase. The combining phase then depends only on the geometric delay: `cos(2π·fc·delay)`.

- Good F2 (delay 1.0 ms, fc 1500 Hz): `cos(2π·1500·0.001) = cos(3π) = −1` → permanent destructive null → 0/20
- Good F1 (delay 0.5 ms, fc 1500 Hz): `cos(3π/2) = 0` → delayed ray orthogonal to I channel → no ISI

Fix: use `h.re × √2` so the random ionospheric phase randomises the combining sign per frame. Differential detection handles frames where the fading sign is consistent; FEC+interleaver corrects burst errors at sign-transition boundaries.

**Result**: `watterson_good_f2_bpsk250_with_fec` now passes. `BPSK250 + RS FEC + interleaver depth 5` correctly decodes through Watterson Good F2 (seed 5).
